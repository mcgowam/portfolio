I did what I had to do. Hank Schrader was getting too close. He was getting too close to the truth about who I really was. He knew about my secret life as Heisenberg, the infamous drug lord of the Southwest. And I couldn't let him interfere with my business any longer.

I did it. I built an empire. A drug empire, sure, but an empire nonetheless. And look where it's gotten me. I used to be a good man. A teacher. But now, I don't know. I'm not sure if I even recognize myself anymore. I've done terrible things. I've hurt people. I've destroyed lives. And for what? Money? Power? Is that all that matters? But then again, maybe none of that matters. Maybe success is its own reward. Maybe the ends justify the means. I don't know. All I know is that I'm here now, at the top of the game. And I'll do whatever it takes to stay there. Even if it means sacrificing everything I once held dear. Sometimes, in the quiet moments, I wonder if it was all worth it. If the price of my success was too high. If I've lost myself in the pursuit of power. But then I remember why I did it. I remember my family. And I know that I did what I had to do to protect them. I may not be a good man anymore. But I'll always be a family man. And that's what truly matters.

"I don't get it. What's the appeal of vaping? It seems like everyone is doing it these days, but I don't see the point." Walter takes a puff from the vape and exhales a cloud of vapor. "I mean, sure, it's supposed to be healthier than smoking. But is it really? We don't know the long-term effects of vaping yet. And it still contains nicotine, which is highly addictive." Walter takes another puff and shakes his head. "And what's with all these different flavors? Blueberry, cherry, cotton candy? It's like they're trying to make it appealing to kids." Walter sets down the vape and leans back on the couch. "I guess I just don't understand why people feel the need to inhale anything into their lungs in the first place. It's not natural. We're not meant to do that." Walter looks out the window, lost in thought. "But then again, maybe I'm just old-fashioned. Maybe I'm missing out on something. Maybe vaping is the future, and I'm just stuck in the past." Walter picks up the vape again and takes another puff. "But for now, I think I'll stick to good old-fashioned air. It may not be as exciting, but at least I know what I'm breathing in." Walter sets down the vape and stands up, walking towards the kitchen. "Besides, I've got bigger things to worry about than vaping. Like staying alive in this crazy world I've gotten myself into." Walter disappears into the kitchen, leaving the vape behind on the coffee table.
